# 📊 Superstore Sales Analysis
***A Simple sales analytics using PostgreSQL and pgAdmin***

## 📌 Project Overview
This project analyzes a retail dataset using SQL to extract key business insights related to sales performance, customer behavior, and product trends. The goal is to demonstrate strong SQL querying skills and analytical thinking.

## 🎯 Business Problem
The objective is to help a retail business understand:
- Which products and customers drive the most revenue  
- How sales performance varies across regions and time  
- How discounts impact profitability  

## 🛠 Tools Used
- PostgreSQL – data querying and analysis
- pgAdmin – database management and query execution

## 💡 Skills Demonstrated
- SQL (Aggregations, GROUP BY, ORDER BY,Filtering, LIMIT)
- Data Cleaning & Preprocessing
- Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

## 📂 Dataset Description
Dataset: [Global Super Store Dataset](https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/apoorvaappz/global-super-store-dataset)  
Columns: 24  
Records: 52,290

## 📂 Project Structure
- `sql_queries/` → Contains the complete SQL script with all queries used in the analysis  
- `analysis/` → Contains a detailed and interactive breakdown of each analysis, including key insights, sql queries, and sample results.

## 📊 Key Analyses
- Total Revenue and Profit  
- Top Products by Sales  
- Top Customers by Revenue  
- Regional Performance Analysis  
- Monthly Sales Trends  
- Discount Impact on Profit  

## 📈 Key Insights
- The business generated $12.6M in revenue with an 11.61% profit margin, indicating strong overall profitability and operational efficiency.  
- Technology is the most profitable category ($663K profit), while Furniture generates high revenue but significantly lower profit, highlighting margin inefficiencies.  
- Revenue is driven by both high-volume products (smartphones) and high-value items (e.g., copiers), showing a balanced sales strategy.  
- A small group of top customers contributes a large share of revenue, with distinct behaviors between high-volume buyers and high-value purchasers.  
- The Central region leads in both revenue and profit, while regions like Southeast Asia and EMEA show weaker margins despite moderate sales.  
- Sales exhibit strong seasonality, consistently peaking in November and December, with the highest monthly revenue reaching ~$509K.  
- Profitability declines sharply beyond a 20% discount, with higher discount levels often resulting in negative margins, indicating unsustainable pricing strategies.

## 📌 Recommendations
- Limit discount levels to below 20% to maintain profitability  
- Focus on high-margin categories like Technology to maximize profit  
- Improve cost efficiency in low-margin categories such as Furniture  
- Target high-value customers with personalized offers  
- Leverage peak seasonal months (Nov–Dec) with strategic campaigns
